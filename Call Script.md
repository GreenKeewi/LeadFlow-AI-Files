# HVAC Meeting Script

### Ad Agency Pitch — Talking Points

---

## 1 — Opener

> Start the conversation

- Thank them for the time, keep it casual — _"I'll keep this short, just want to show you what we're doing"_
- Ask how business has been lately — genuinely listen, this sets up your pitch naturally
- Ask what their slow season looks like and how they usually handle it

---

## 2 — The Problem

> Make them feel the gap

- Most HVAC companies rely on referrals — great when it works, brutal when it doesn't
- Agencies that actually specialize in HVAC charge **$2,000–$3,000+/month** just for the retainer, ad spend on top
- They pay whether they get results or not — no accountability
- Most businesses also have a goldmine sitting in their old customer list that nobody's touching

---

## 3 — What We Do

> Walk through the features casually

- **Paid ads (Google + Meta)** — targeted to your area, the jobs you want, managed every week not just set-and-forget
- **Reactivation campaigns** ⭐ — we take your old customer list and send automated SMS + email sequences to bring them back. One campaign can book 10–20 jobs from people who already trust you, almost zero ad spend
- **Seasonal pushes** — campaigns ready before summer AC season and winter heating season so you're never scrambling
- **Lead follow-up automation** — when a lead comes in, they get a text and email within minutes. Most HVAC companies lose jobs just from slow response
- **Reputation management** — we prompt review requests after every job. More reviews = lower ad costs and more organic calls
- **Monthly reporting** — leads, bookings, what's working. No fluff
- **Custom Seasonal Ads** — custom ads that change to the season.

---

## 4 — Performance Model

> The part that makes them lean in

- On top of management, you pay **$20 per lead that makes you money** — not per click, not per form fill, per converted job
- Average HVAC service call is $200–400. Install is $3,000–10,000. You're paying $20 for a customer worth 100x that
- If the lead doesn't convert, you don't pay for it — we only do well when you do well

---

## 5 — Objections

> Have these ready, don't over-explain

- **"We already get referrals"** — referrals are great, this adds a consistent layer on top. Referrals fluctuate, this doesn't
- **"We tried ads before, didn't work"** — usually means someone set it up and walked away. We actively manage it every week
- **"I don't have time for this"** — you won't need to. You get the leads, we handle everything else
- **"Is there a contract?"** — no long-term commitment to start. Run a month, see the results, then decide

---

## 6 — The Close

> Drop the price last

- Recap everything — ads, reactivation, automation, seasonal campaigns, reputation, reporting — _"all of that, done for you"_
- Remind them competitors charge $2,000–$3,000/month for less

---

### 💰 $4000 / month

**+ $20 per converted lead · no long-term contract · everything included**
But remember, you can split that cost across many dealers. 

---

## 7 — Prep Questions

> Know the answers before you walk in

- What's their average job ticket? _(frames your ROI math)_
- How big is their past customer list? _(bigger list = stronger reactivation pitch)_
- Are they running any ads right now — and what's their cost per lead? _(you need to beat it)_
- How do they follow up with leads today? _(most have terrible follow-up — this is your opening)_
- How many Google reviews do they have and what's their rating? _(reputation management urgency)_
- What's their busiest service — installs, repairs, or maintenance? _(tailor which campaign to lead with)_
- When's their slow season? _(reactivation campaigns solve this directly)_
